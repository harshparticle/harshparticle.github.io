I am a second-year PhD student in theoretical and computational chemistry at the University of Southern California. My research focuses on non-radiative decay processes in organic chromophores, including internal conversion and intersystem crossing, with an emphasis on vibronic coupling and rate theories.

Research

Broadly, I am interested in understanding how molecular structure and vibrational motion control electronic relaxation pathways in organic chromophores. My work combines electronic structure theory, vibronic models, and rate theories based on Fermi's golden rule.

Research Areas

  • Non-radiative decay in organic chromophores (e.g., azulene derivatives, spiropyran/merocyanine systems).
  • Time-dependent approaches to internal conversion and intersystem crossing using thermal vibrational correlation functions.
  • Development and application of ezFCF for vibronic structure and non-radiative rate calculations.

Current Directions

  • Implementing and benchmarking time-dependent FCWD-based internal conversion rate calculations using effective-mode models.
  • Analyzing robustness of excitation-energy transfer in model systems inspired by photosynthetic complexes.
  • Exploring solvent and environment effects on non-radiative pathways via reduced models and kinetic descriptions.
