Research

I am broadly interested in how molecular structure and vibrational motion control electronic relaxation pathways in organic chromophores. My work combines electronic structure theory, vibronic models, and rate theories based on Fermi's golden rule.

Main Research Areas

  • Non-radiative decay in organic chromophores (e.g., azulene derivatives, spiropyran/merocyanine systems).
  • Time-dependent internal conversion and intersystem crossing using thermal vibrational correlation functions.
  • Development and application of ezFCF for vibronic structure and non-radiative rate calculations.

Current Projects

  • Implementing and benchmarking time-dependent FCWD-based internal conversion rates using effective-mode models.
  • Studying robustness of excitation-energy transfer in model systems inspired by photosynthetic complexes.
  • Exploring solvent and environment effects on non-radiative pathways via reduced models and kinetic descriptions.
